Conor Dyson

PhD Student | Center of Gravity, Niels Bohr Institute

Well! I'm Conor Dyson, a PhD Student origionaly from the border of Tipperary and Kilkenny, Ireland, now based in Copenhagen. I am pursuing a PhD at the Niels Bohr Institute, University of Copenhagen where I am supervised by Maarten van de Meent and Vitor Cardoso. My primary interests are in post-adiabatic gravitational self-force for modeling intermediary and extreme mass ratio inspirals. Beyond modeling binary black holes in the purely gravitational case, my interest in these systems also extends to modeling these systems in the presence of environments. Beyond physics I a spend the majority of my free time playing rugby at the CSR Nanok rugbyclub in Christianhavn, listening to and playing music and general enjoying a few beers and spending time with friend in Sunny Copenhagen.

Research

My work focuses on gravitational self-force in both vacuum spacetimes and those with material environments. I aim to develop self-consistent models for the evolution of small bodies in curved spacetimes, with an eye toward physical interpretation and numerical tractability.
