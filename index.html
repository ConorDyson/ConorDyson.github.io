Well! For some reason you've landed on my personal webpage, I've no idea what could've brought you here but now that you've arrived I may as well tell you a bit about myself. I'm Conor Dyson, originally from Poulacapple East on the border of Tipperary and Kilkenny, Ireland. I’m now based in Copenhagen, Denmark, where I'm pursuing my PhD at the Niels Bohr Institute, University of Copenhagen. My interests broadly lie in black hole perturbation theory but more specifically in applying the self-force formalism to model extreme and intermediary mass ratio inspirals in the strong gravity regime. Be that in terms of including the leading order non-linear effects of the self-force or in terms of including the effects of the environment on the motion of the small body. So if any of take a seat back and relax, I hope you enjoy your stay!

Research

My work focuses on gravitational self-force in both vacuum spacetimes and those with material environments. I aim to develop self-consistent models for the evolution of small bodies in curved spacetimes, with an eye toward physical interpretation and numerical tractability.
