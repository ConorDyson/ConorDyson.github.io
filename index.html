Conor Dyson

PhD Student | Center of Gravity, Niels Bohr Institute

Well! I'm Conor Dyson, originally from Poulacapple East on the border of Tipperary and Kilkenny, Ireland. I’m now based in Copenhagen, Denmark, where I pursue my PhD at the Niels Bohr Institute, University of Copenhagen. I’m supervised by Maarten van de Meent and Vitor Cardoso.

My primary research interest is in post-adiabatic gravitational self-force for modeling intermediate and extreme mass-ratio inspirals. I'm particularly focused on binary black hole systems — not only in vacuum but also in the presence of environments.

Outside of physics, I spend most of my free time playing rugby with CSR Nanok in Christianshavn, listening to and playing music, and enjoying a few beers with friends under the Copenhagen sun.

Research

My work focuses on gravitational self-force in both vacuum spacetimes and those with material environments. I aim to develop self-consistent models for the evolution of small bodies in curved spacetimes, with an eye toward physical interpretation and numerical tractability.
